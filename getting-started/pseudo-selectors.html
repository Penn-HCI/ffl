Pseudo-Selectors

FFL currently only offers one single pseudo-selector.

:nth(…) Selector

For most non-CSS selectors in FFL, it is possible to specify the occurrence of the selector in the formula. For example, $x$:nth(0) matches the first occurrence of \(x\) in the formula and $x$:nth(1) matches the second occurrence. Note that the index is currently zero-based, this might change in the future.

y=ax^2+bx+c

This is often useful with .group selector, which selects parts of your formula surrounded in double curly braces ({…}).

a{{b}}{{a}}

Note that .group:nth(0) means the global group, which is the entire formula, so .group isn't usually useful on its own. (This might change and .group indices might become 1-based.) And using the intersection selector together the :nth selector is currently undefined, although you can usually expect it to attach to the adjacent basic selector.
